iPhones Sold

iPhone sales drive over 60% of total net revenues for Apple. The company announced the iPhone X in September 2017 featuring facial recognition. iPhone is based on Apple’s iOS operating system and includes Siri, a voice-activated assistant, Apple pay and touch ID.

Average Selling Price

The average selling price of Apple’s iPhones are important in driving revenues since iPhones represent over 60% of the company’s revenues. For instance, iPhone net sales increased in 2017 compared to 2016 due to higher iPhone unit sales and a different mix of iPhones with higher average selling prices.

Service Revenue

Services includes the iTunes store, App store, Mac App store, TV App store, iBooks store, and Apple Music. Services represented ~13% of total net revenues in 2017, experiencing 23% revenue growth in 2017 and 22% revenue growth in 2016. This growth was driven mainly through increases in App store and licensing sales.
